# Day 01 - Basic Algebra

## What is Algebra?

Algebra is a branch of mathematics that uses symbols and variables to represent numbers and relationships.

Example:

x + 5 = 10

Here, x is a variable whose value we need to find.

---

## Why Algebra is Important for Data Analysts

Data Analysts frequently work with:

* Business formulas
* Statistical equations
* DAX calculations in Power BI
* Machine Learning formulas
* Data transformations

Understanding algebra makes it easier to interpret analytical models and business metrics.

---

## Key Concepts

### Variables

A variable is a symbol that represents an unknown value.

Examples:

x
y
sales
profit

---

### Constants

A constant is a fixed value.

Examples:

5
100
3.14

---

### Algebraic Expressions

Examples:

x + 5

2x + 10

3x - y

---

### Equations

An equation states that two expressions are equal.

Example:

x + 5 = 10

Solution:

x = 10 - 5

x = 5

---

## Order of Operations (BODMAS)

1. Brackets
2. Orders (Powers)
3. Division
4. Multiplication
5. Addition
6. Subtraction

Example:

(5 + 3) × 2

= 8 × 2

= 16

---

## Real World Example

Revenue = Price × Quantity

If:

Price = ₹500

Quantity = 20

Revenue = 500 × 20 = ₹10,000

This simple equation is algebra.
